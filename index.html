A Recognition Spoken in Peace

I want you to be the one in a way that goes beyond reason. Something in me recognized you long before I tried to explain it. Loving you never felt like a choice, I didn't fall in love alone. Love fell into me before my conscious mind could name it. It entered quietly, moved through me like returning to a home I didn't remember leaving, like a memory from before memory existed. You felt familiar because you spoke to parts of me I didn't know I had lived with my whole life. And in giving it room, I realized I wasn't just feeling love, I was becoming it, because with you I could be anything, and everything it asked of me. It chose me as its place, and in finding somewhere to stay, it took your shape, because somehow, from the very beginning, it had known your name.

You aren't just someone I love, you are my someone, for life. There were moments when loving you asked more of me than comfort. I didn't always get it right. I know there were times when I failed you, especially when your love was most open and mine was less careful than it should have been. I only came to understand through the pain I caused you. You stayed until I learned, and I promise to stay forever. I stopped wishing you into permanence and began creating it, not through hope alone, but through loving you in a way that changed how I live, how I listen, how I stay. Not only because the thought of losing you scared me, but because understanding what you deserve made loving you feel like the most natural way to move through the world, and love became a way of being.

What we have now feels quieter, and that quiet is not absence, it is peace. Loving you no longer feels like holding my breath for the waves, but like laying beside the ocean once the tide has settled. You are not a dream I keep chasing. You are a dream come true, a presence that doesn't ask me to prove anything, only to be honest and steady. The future doesn't resist us anymore because it's no longer blurry. It's shared between us, built from small moments that don't need witnesses to be meaningful. The quiet choices, the everyday care, the moments that matter when no one is watching.

If the future feels possible to me now, it's because you're in it. Not as an idea, not as a hope I have to force into shape, but as a truth that unfolds naturally, quietly, steadily. I feel you meeting me here. I feel you choosing this too. I feel your care in it. I see the way you're showing me.

So this is not a promise made in excitement. It's a recognition spoken in peace.
